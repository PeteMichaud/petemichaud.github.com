Do I contradict myself? Very well, then I contradict myself, I am large, I contain multitudes.
-Walt Whitman

I'm going to tell you who I am, and who I am not. You'll know I'm telling the truth because it's not all pretty.

First and foremost, I love people. Aside from technology, I've spent the most time learning about people: psychology, communication, relationships. If you want to understand why people do what they do, or you want to figure out how to convince them to do something else, I'm your man. A side effect is that I'm incredibly easy to get along with; I'm easy to like and I work hard to understand and listen hard.

I love helping. If I hadn't gotten a handle on it when I was a young adult, my drive to help would be pathological. When I was young I would commit to helping everyone, doing their charity projects, editing their manuscripts, connecting them to the right people. And often, I'd fail them because I was over extended, stressed out, and I'd drop the ball. I can put you in touch with people today who will tell you I still have a dangerous "helpful" streak when it comes to giving too much and demanding too little. I'm still on the fence about whether it's really a liability.

I like technology. I'm excited by the impact it can make on our lives, and I'm proud to be among the relatively few people (along with my fellow geeks) who can understand, follow, and occasionally contribute to the latest developments.

But look, I don't love technology. When I was a kid, everyone assumed I'd grow up to be a software developer because that was the most employable of the things I was consistently interested in (as opposed to, say, art). But I'd correct them, and say I only think it's fun, I don't really have a passion for it, in and of itself. I most often used it to meet some other goal of mine. If you had asked me when I was 11 what I wanted to do when I grow up, I would've answered "Save the World." The answer never really changed.

In other words, I don't give a shit about the first world problem your startup is solving. I enjoy the technology, I really understand the hard work that goes into a startup, and I hope you make a trillion dollars. I might even do something similar again one day for my own trillion, if I think it'll help me save the world. Maybe even with you, if you convince me you can sell it.

But frankly, cool technology isn't enough for me. I like playing with my toys as much as the next geek, but if you're going to ask me to spend my life doing something, it better be to create a better world. Like I said, I have a nearly pathological need to help, on both a local and global scale.

If I like you and I think your tech is cool, I'll come play with you for three to six months maybe. And I'll add a shit load of value while I'm there. I've been responsible for multimillion dollar projects that would ensure my victory in any game of buzzword bingo. I've started a successful internet company (and lots more failed ones). I've worked deeply in several stacks including Ruby on Rails, .NET, and even PHP (that'll cost you extra, baby). I'm a ninja guru pirate whatever on the client side, since most of my work has been on the web, and that's the common denominator.

I am a guy who can design your user experience and information architecture, build your backend in practically whatever technology you want, then do the statistical analysis on your user base and present the findings at a conference.

I am not a guy you want to hire to maintain your widget that connects advertisers to their dogs. That won't end well for anyone.

I'm the guy you want to hire if you're trying to save the world and you need someone who has the fire and breadth of interest and skill to help you make that happen.
